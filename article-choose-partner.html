How to Choose the Best AV Partner for Your Next Event

Posted in Event Planning

Choosing the right AV partner can make or break your event's success. It's a decision that goes far beyond a simple quote for equipment; it's about finding a team that becomes a seamless extension of your own, a partner invested in your outcome. But in a crowded market, how do you separate the vendors from the true partners? It comes down to asking the right questions and knowing what to look for.

1. Look Beyond the Quote: It's About Partnership, Not Price

The lowest bid is rarely the best value. A vendor simply provides boxes and bodies. A true partner provides expertise, accountability, and peace of mind. They operate with a hospitality mindset, understanding that their job is to make you look good and ensure your audience has a flawless experience. They invest time upfront to understand your goals, and they're the calm, steady hand you can rely on when the pressure is on.

2. Ask the Right Questions

Any company can send you a list of equipment. To find a real partner, you need to dig deeper. Here are three essential questions to ask any potential AV company:

  • "What is your process?" - Look for a team that has a disciplined, structured approach. A great partner will be able to walk you through their process, from discovery and design to pre-production and on-site execution. (You can see our process here).
  • "Who, specifically, will be on-site at my event?" - You want to know that you'll have seasoned, professional technicians, not just entry-level staff. The experience of the on-site team is one of the most critical factors in an event's success.
  • "Can you show me examples of events you've done that are similar in scope to mine?" - A confident partner will be proud to show you their work and connect you with past clients. Their portfolio should give you a clear sense of their capabilities and quality.
"A great AV team doesn't just provide gear; they provide peace of mind. That's the real value."

3. Watch for the Red Flags

Just as important as knowing what to look for is knowing what to avoid. Be wary of companies that provide vague quotes with potential hidden fees, are slow to respond to your questions, or seem more interested in pushing specific equipment than in understanding your event's goals. A lack of transparency and communication in the planning phase is a major red flag for how they'll perform on-site.
